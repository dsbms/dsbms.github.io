idea: "A standalone tool that compares whole lipidomes by their structural and quantitative differences."
lead: "Comparing thousands of distinct lipidomes is hard. LipidSpace builds a structural space from the lipids themselves, then uses it to measure how similar two lipidomes are — turning a pile of tables into an interpretable map of an experiment."
concepts:
  - "Graph-based comparison of lipid structures yields a structural space model."
  - "Lipidome similarity is computed from distances within that space."
  - "Machine-learning selects lipid subsets that explain study variables such as health condition."
findings:
  - "Enables rapid (re)analysis and quality control across large-scale studies."
  - "Identifies the lipids that shape each lipidome."
  - "Reads Goslin-normalized names and imports mzTab-M; GUI with tutorials and PDF export."
