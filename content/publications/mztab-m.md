chip: "mzTab-M"

year: "2019"
venue: "Analytical Chemistry"
authors: "Hoffmann N, Rein J, Sachsenberg T, Hartler J, et al."
doi: "10.1021/acs.analchem.8b04310"
idea: "A single, well-specified text format for sharing quantitative metabolomics and lipidomics results."
lead: "Results that cannot be exchanged cannot be reused. mzTab-M defines a reporting format that is simple to read yet rigorously specified, so quantitative small-molecule results travel cleanly between tools, repositories and labs."
concepts:
  - "A tab-separated format whose structure is fixed by a detailed specification."
  - "Metadata, summary and feature/evidence sections capture an experiment end-to-end."
  - "Co-developed by the HUPO Proteomics and the Metabolomics Standards Initiatives."
findings:
  - "Provides reproducible, machine-readable reporting for metabolomics and lipidomics."
  - "Adopted by a range of software, enabling real interoperability."
  - "Serves as a direct import format for downstream tools such as LipidSpace."
