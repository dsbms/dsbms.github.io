idea: "A points-based scoring system that gives each lipid identification a transparent quality and confidence score from the analytical evidence behind it."
lead: "Lipidomics studies report identifications with very different levels of supporting evidence, yet that confidence is rarely captured in a comparable way. EPoS-MoL introduces an empirical point-score model that rewards the analytical information behind each identification — MS, MS/MS, chromatography and ion mobility — to express data quality on a common scale. It is available both as an R package and as an interactive web application."
concepts:
  - "Points are awarded across complementary analytical layers — accurate mass, fragmentation, chromatography and ion mobility."
  - "The resulting score expresses identification confidence on a single, comparable scale."
  - "Delivered as an R package, a Shiny web app and a Docker container for easy reuse."
findings:
  - "A transparent, community-oriented scheme for scoring lipid identification quality."
  - "Consistent quality assessment that travels across tools, studies and laboratories."
  - "Open-source implementation with an interactive app at apps.lifs-tools.org/p/app/eposmol."
